Smartphone holder

I wanted to create a phone holder that fit the most common smartphones and not one that is only designed for one phone in particular. I also wanted to be able to place the phone horizontally and vertically in the holder

More generally I made this because I wanted a phone holder which was strong enough to support pressure in order to enable the phone to be used while placed in the phone holder. I discovered that many phone stands are designed simply to place the phone in it and perhaps play a video or music from it while placed. Or just for the aesthetics. I wanted more functionality, and I wanted to be able to use my phone, browsing, scrolling or viewing things while working at the computer. Hence why I created a phone holder which enables the user to interact with it without the phone falling out of the holder or the holder tipping over itself.

Info

Smartphone holder developed with Autodesk Maya, Rhinoceros 3D and printed with Makerbot replicator 2x

Task

Create a solid smartphone holder which can handle some amount of force put on the touch screen

Approach

Three parts

Early stage image of the model in Rhino. Unfortunately I had no screenshot.

The holder was printed in four separate parts. The base, the back and two feet that elevates the phone. I designed that holder with a hole at the bottom in between the feet, which in combination with the elevation allows me to plug in my charger (can be seen in image 4 & 5).

Summary

A serious shortcoming of our proposal is that core-Wikipedians will most certainly be reluctant to implement our suggestions. As we have seen with the visual editor it is very hard for the Wikimedia foundation to propose changes to their user base. We know therefore that it is quite unlikely that they will be successful in persuading their users of a redesign of the platform. However, we still decided to at least create an initial redesign concept to give a clearer picture of what we mean by improving the accessibility of the site. We believe that by lowering the technical barriers Wikipedia would be successful in attracting a more diverse user group and that would benefit the society because there would be a much broader knowledge for everyone available on Wikipedia.
