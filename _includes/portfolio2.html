Wikipedia redesign

When looking at Wikipedia with the lens of accessibility, as it stands, there is much to be desired. The main menu is longer than most articles, the featured links on the main page are just big boxes of text, many features are hidden behind secret search syntax and some buttons have ambiguous names without context, making it hard to tell what they do. Wikipedia is made upon a classic hyperlink hierarchy, going from one article to the next seamlessly, but the result can be confusing to new users, as most of the site uses the same structure and templates. User pages look exactly the same as articles, for example. There are also important community guidelines and rules that are hidden to all but experienced users. In the testing of the site, one of my newly created articles got removed because of relevance guidelines that I had never heard of, and that was specific to that specific language version of Wikipedia.

Info

This project took place at the department of Informatics, Umeå University, Spring 2015.

Background

The main issue that Wikipedia faces is the decline in active contributors, also known as editors. They are the core of Wikipedia and without them articles are not written, translated or reviewed. Another issue is the lack of diversity, where 80% of the users are male. This leads to "female" topics not being covered to the same extent as typical "male" topics. In addition to this, the core users (editors) are reluctant to change and normal users (readers) are not keen on becoming editors.

Challenge

Develop and present a design proposal in which current users input is considered but also a design that invites new users.

Role

UX designer, Interaction designer and Visual designer.

Approach

The approach focused on bringing accessibility to Wikipedia. The social dimensions are already implemented but they are hard to find and seldom used. In terms of navigation and overall interface design, it is poorly developed and hinders the interaction between users, hence the focus on accessibility, rather than adding new social features that are not needed or unwanted by the community.

Main Page

One of the first major changes we decided upon was to recede the size of the main menu. It is easy to make it smaller, using an accordion, so that menus can be expanded and collapsed. One of our research findings was, that knowing about specific topic areas which needed help would turn non-contributors into participants. We decided that this was an important feature to make people both aware of what they can do, but also encouraging them to do so by making it more accessible. So we added “Take part!” to the menu, with prominent green background, an overview about different possibilities of taking part. We also changed the “Interaction” menu to “Community” instead, as that is what you’re interacting with - the community.

To deal with the hidden search syntax issue on Wikipedia, we added a feature that already exists on the Wikimedia Commons site. We added, beneath the search function, options to search in specific parts of the site. By adding this search options and moving the search next to the Wikipedia logo, we increase availability, by increasing awareness and accessibility

Another issue was the cluttered main page, where all kinds of featured texts crowd one another. Even though all these features are something we want to make more prominent, we also want to make them more attractive and less intimidating. We chose to design them similarly to common widgets on other sites
